Mortar Watchtower

Instant, local feedback for Apache Pig.

Developing for Hadoop is a pain. Unlike software engineers, data scientists have to wait hours to see if their code worked. I imagine it's akin to writing code on punch cards. That's why I came up with Watchtower – a tool that evaluates your Apache Pig scripts locally, instantly feeding you the results.

Too often when writing scripts for Hadoop, your script fails after 8 hours because of a one-off error in the final UDF. Watchtower flushes out those errors instantly by running subsets of your data through your entire script.

Since launching this project with Mortar Data in July 2013, people have become pretty excited about it. You can see what they're saying here.

Lambda Playground

A Lambda Calculus step reducer.

In my 3A term at the University of Waterloo, I decided to take principles of programming languages. Initially I was drawn to it because I'm a huge fan of the professor, Prabhakar Ragde, but the content drew me in pretty quickly there after.

I was trying to come up with the predecessor function for Church numerals when I found myself fumbling over Normal Order Reduction. I built this tool to help illustrate different reduction strategies in order to better understand things like Church numerals and the notorious Y-Combinator.
